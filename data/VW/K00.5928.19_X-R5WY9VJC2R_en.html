Removing and installing brake caliper piston

Special tools and workshop equipment required
  • Piston resetting appliance T10145
  • Removal tool T40338
  • Removal tool T10498
Removing
  • Remove brake caliper Chapter“Removing and installing brake caliper”.
  • Remove brake pads Chapter“Removing and installing brake pads”.
  • Clamp brake caliper in a vice using protective jaw covers.
  • When removing brake caliper pistons and protective caps, make sure to not damage the paint layer on the brake caliper.
  • Only one brake caliper piston can be pressed out at a time.
  • Use piston resetting appliance T10145 A to hold opposite brake caliper piston in brake caliper 1.
  • To avoid damaging paint layer on brake caliper, place a piece of rubber between piston resetting appliance T10145 A and brake caliper.
  • Block the other brake caliper pistons with wooden blocks C. Place a means of protection B in front of piston resetting appliance T10145 A so that the brake caliper piston is not damaged when being pressed out.
  • Fit nozzle of compressed air gun D on threaded hole for brake hose.
  • Press brake caliper piston 3 out of brake caliper using compressed air.
  • Use removal tool T40338 to lever protective cap 4 out of brake caliper 1 by applying slight lever movements. Do not damage groove in brake caliper when doing this.
  • Remove seal 2 using release tool T10498.
  • Make sure not to damage the surface of the cylinder.
  • Repeat procedure for the other brake caliper pistons.
Installing
  • Clean surfaces of piston and seal using only methylated spirits and then dry them.
  • Check brake caliper piston and cylinder wall for damage.
  • Install complete repair kit.
  • Note different diameters of brake caliper pistons.
  • Apply a thin coat of assembly paste to seal before inserting Electronic parts catalogue (ETKA).
  • Insert seal in brake caliper, and make sure it is properly seated.
  • Apply a thin coat of assembly paste to seal after inserting Electronic parts catalogue (ETKA).
  • Fit protective caps 4 on brake caliper pistons 3.
  • The protective cap 4 must be properly seated in groove arrow.
  • Apply a thin coat of assembly paste to piston and seal before inserting Electronic parts catalogue (ETKA).
  • Push brake caliper piston 3 into brake caliper 1 by applying pressure evenly, and make sure to keep brake caliper piston straight.
  • Push protective cap 4 into groove of brake caliper.
  • The protective cap must be firmly seated in groove arrow.
  • Gently re-press using piston resetting appliance T10145.
  • Repeat procedure on next brake caliper piston.
